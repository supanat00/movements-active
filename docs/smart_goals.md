# SMART Goals

## When to Use This Skill
Use when planning a project, setting performance metrics, or defining the scope of an upcoming sprint.

## When Not to Use This Skill
Avoid for vague, long-term vision statements or exploratory research where outcomes are completely unknown.

## Goal
Ensure objectives are clear, trackable, and realistic to improve focus and accountability.

## Workflow
1. **Specific:** Clearly state what needs to be accomplished and by whom.
2. **Measurable:** Define concrete metrics to track progress and success.
3. **Achievable:** Ensure the goal is realistic given current resources and constraints.
4. **Relevant:** Verify that the goal aligns with broader business objectives.
5. **Time-bound:** Set a clear deadline or timeframe for completion.

## Validation
- The goal answers the 5 elements of SMART.
- Success or failure can be objectively determined at the deadline.

## Safety Rules
- Do not set goals that rely heavily on factors outside the team's control.
- Do not use vague metrics like "improve" or "better" without baseline numbers.

## Output Format
A clearly formatted SMART goal statement, broken down by its 5 components.

## References
- Project requirements, team OKRs.

## Stop and Escalate If
- The goal is deemed unachievable due to resource constraints.
- The metrics required for "Measurable" cannot be tracked.
