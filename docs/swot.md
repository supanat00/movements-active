# SWOT Analysis

## When to Use This Skill
Use during strategic planning, before launching a new product, or when evaluating a significant architectural shift.

## When Not to Use This Skill
Avoid for minor, routine decisions or highly technical debugging tasks.

## Goal
Provide a comprehensive overview of internal capabilities and external factors to inform strategic decision-making.

## Workflow
1. **Strengths:** Identify internal advantages, resources, and unique capabilities.
2. **Weaknesses:** Identify internal limitations, resource gaps, and areas for improvement.
3. **Opportunities:** Identify external trends, market gaps, or favorable conditions.
4. **Threats:** Identify external risks, competitors, or unfavorable market shifts.
5. Synthesize findings to suggest strategic actions (e.g., use strengths to mitigate threats).

## Validation
- Includes both internal (S, W) and external (O, T) factors.
- Factors are factual and based on data, not just assumptions.

## Safety Rules
- Do not confuse internal weaknesses with external threats.
- Be brutally honest about weaknesses.

## Output Format
A 2x2 grid or structured list detailing S-W-O-T, followed by actionable strategic recommendations.

## References
- Market research, competitor analysis, internal audits.

## Stop and Escalate If
- Data is severely lacking to accurately assess external Opportunities or Threats.
